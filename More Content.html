BMI (BODY MASS INDEX)

What is BMI?

Body mass index (BMI) is a tool that healthcare providers use to estimate how much body fat you have by measuring your height and weight. It can be useful in assessing risk factors for certain health conditions.

For most people, BMI is correlated with body fat (the higher the number, the more fat you may have), but in some cases it is not very accurate. BMI alone does not determine health.

What is BMI used for?

Health care providers use BMI and other tools and tests to assess health status and safety risks in people. Too much body fat can lead to heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes. Too little body fat can be linked to malnutrition. This index helps determine the right amount of body fat relative to vitamins and minerals. It also determines how much energy your body uses, helps maintain body temperature, and protects your organs from obesity.
